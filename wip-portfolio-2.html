Hello there! I’m Yomna, a Technology Fellow at the EFF (Electronic Frontier Foundation), and a software engineer at Stripe. I have a degree in mathematics from the University of Waterloo.

At the EFF, I’ve worked on building tools to encrypt the web, fighting privacy-invasive ads, measuring the progress of AI research, and techniques to overcome cell network surveillance. At Stripe I work on building scalable infrastructure using functional programming.

My research interests lie at the intersection of technology (especially cryptography!), society, and social justice.

Projects

Certbot (previously “the Let’s Encrypt client”) is an open source project by the EFF to acquire and install SSL certificates primarily from Let’s Encrypt. Cerbot & Let’s Encrypt have helped secure at least 47 million domains and have helped the Web go from 40% to 58% encrypted page loads.
