Hello there! I’m Yomna, a Technology Fellow at the EFF (Electronic Frontier Foundation), and a software engineer at Stripe. I have a degree in mathematics from the University of Waterloo.

At the EFF, I’ve worked on building tools to encrypt the web, fighting privacy-invasive ads, measuring the progress of AI research, and techniques to overcome cell network surveillance. At Stripe I work on building scalable infrastructure using functional programming.

My research interests lie at the intersection of technology and society, especially (1) building technology informed by social justice to empower users, (2) using technology to create art that encourages empathy and curiosity. I’m especially passionate about both theoretical and applied cryptography, as well as art.

Projects

Certbot (previously the "Let's Encrypt client") Historically, acquiring and installing SSL certificates has been an expensive, challenging process, leading to many sites only supporting insecure HTTP. As was revealed a few years ago in the Snowden leaks, many governments and ISPs are engaged in unethical mass surveillance, and one way to fight back against this dragnet surveillance has been to encourage the adoption of HTTPS.
